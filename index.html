About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Operations and Decision Technologies department at the Kelley School of Business, Indiana University (advised by George Ball and Owen Wu).

My research examines the intersection of sustainable operations, behavioral operations, and people-centric operations. Broadly, I study how a firm's operational decisions in pursuit of environmental and/or social goals shape operational performance. A growing focus of my work is how these operational practices interact with worker behavior to generate benefits and/or trade-offs that emerge in practice. I employ econometric and data-driven empirical methods to produce insights relevant to managerial and policy decisions.

I received a MBus in Operations Management and Decision Sciences from Kelley School of Business, Indiana University, a MS in Operations Management from Seoul National University (advised by Michael Lim), and a BS in Logistics & Industrial Engineering from Inha University (top in graduating class).
