Teaching

BUS-K201: Foundations of Business Information Systems and Decision Making

Role: Full-time Instructor (hired as a Kelley adjunct faculty)

Term: Fall 2026 (Class Size: TBA)

Level: Undergraduate

The course provides a foundation in business information systems, decision making, and emerging technologies. Students will gain practical experience working with relational databases, spreadsheets, data visualization, and business intelligence tools essential to manage and analyze data effectively in business environments.

BUS-K303: Technology & Business Analysis

Role: Full-time Instructor

Term: Fall 2025 (Class Size: 46), Spring 2025 (Class Size: 32)

Level: Undergraduate

An introduction to the ways that technology enables and empowers business decision making. In this introductory analytics course, you will learn how companies apply technology tools to prepare, analyze, model and display the information used to manage their business. You will apply these same techniques using spreadsheet modeling and other tools.

Teaching Interests

  • Operations Management
  • Supply Chain Management
  • Business Analytics
  • Data-driven Decision Making
  • Econometrics
